Pre-Operative Consultation

What is hair transplant surgery?

Hair transplant surgery is a minimally invasive procedure that extracts healthy hair follicles from the occipital region (donor area) of your own head, separates and cultures them, and then implants them into the balding area. The main technologies currently include FUE (Follicular Unit Extraction) and microneedle hair transplant technology.

Barley Microneedle Hair Transplant uses advanced microneedle technology, which has the advantages of small trauma, fast recovery, and natural results. Because the transplanted follicles come from your own occipital region (an area not affected by androgens), they will not fall out again after surviving.

Professional Tip

It is recommended to undergo hair transplant surgery during the stable period of hair loss to achieve better long-term results.

Am I suitable for hair transplant surgery?

Hair transplant surgery is suitable for the following people:

  • Androgenetic alopecia patients - Male pattern baldness (M-type, O-type), female diffuse hair loss
  • Scarring alopecia - Hair loss caused by trauma, surgery, burns, etc.
  • Hairline adjustment - Naturally high hairline or wanting to adjust hairline shape
  • Hair density - Density planting in sparse hair areas
  • Artistic planting - Eyebrows, beard, sideburns, body hair, etc.

However, the following situations require caution: being in the rapid hair loss period, insufficient donor area, suffering from serious diseases, blood diseases, etc. It is recommended to come to the hospital for professional testing.

What preparations do I need to make before hair transplant surgery?

Preparations before hair transplant surgery:

  • Medical history - Inform the doctor of your medical history, allergies, and medications you are taking
  • Avoid alcohol - Stop drinking alcohol one week before surgery
  • Stop taking certain medications - Stop taking aspirin, vitamin E, and other medications that may increase bleeding one week before surgery
  • Wash your hair - Wash your hair thoroughly the night before surgery
  • Wear comfortable clothes - Wear loose, button-down clothes on the day of surgery
  • Avoid intense exercise - Avoid intense exercise one week before surgery
  • Eat normally - Eat normally on the day of surgery, do not fast
Professional Tip

If you have any questions about medications, please consult your doctor in advance.

Is hair transplant suitable for young people?

Hair transplant can be suitable for young people, but the following factors need to be considered:

  • Age - Generally recommended to be over 18 years old
  • Stability of hair loss - It is very important that hair loss has stabilized
  • Donor area resources - Sufficient healthy follicles in the donor area
  • Psychological maturity - Having reasonable expectations

For young people with unstable hair loss, we usually recommend medication first to control hair loss, and then consider hair transplant surgery after the hair loss has stabilized.

Surgery Related

Is hair transplant surgery painful?

Hair transplant surgery is performed under local anesthesia, so you will not feel pain during the surgery. You may feel a slight stinging sensation during the local anesthesia injection, but this is usually mild and tolerable.

During the surgery, you can listen to music, watch TV, or even take a nap. After the anesthesia wears off, you may feel mild discomfort or swelling, which can usually be relieved with over-the-counter painkillers.

Barley's microneedle technology is minimally invasive, and the postoperative reaction is even milder. Most patients can return to work in 2-3 days.

How long does hair transplant surgery take?

The duration of hair transplant surgery depends on the number of follicles to be transplanted:

  • Small area (1000-1500 follicles) - about 3-4 hours
  • Medium area (1500-2500 follicles) - about 4-6 hours
  • Large area (2500-3500 follicles) - about 6-8 hours
  • Extra large area (3500+ follicles) - may require two surgeries

The surgery is performed on an outpatient basis, and you can go home the same day. We will arrange regular breaks during the surgery, and you can eat, drink, and use the restroom.

What is the difference between FUE and microneedle?

Both FUE and microneedle are hair transplant technologies, but there are some differences:

  • Instruments - FUE uses forceps for implantation, while microneedle uses a specialized implant pen
  • Incision size - Microneedle incisions are smaller (0.6-1.0mm), about 1/3 smaller than traditional FUE
  • Recovery time - Microneedle recovers faster, and you can wash your hair in 24 hours
  • Naturalness - Microneedle can rotate 360° to better match the natural hair growth direction
  • Density - Microneedle can achieve higher planting density
  • Follicle damage - Microneedle avoids secondary damage to follicles from forceps

Barley is a pioneer and leader in microneedle hair transplant technology in China, with rich clinical experience.

How many follicles need to be transplanted?

The number of follicles that need to be transplanted varies from person to person and depends on the following factors:

  • Area of hair loss - The larger the area, the more follicles are needed
  • Expected density - The higher the expected density, the more follicles are needed
  • Donor area resources - The number of follicles that can be extracted from the donor area
  • Hair thickness - Coarse hair can achieve a fuller appearance with fewer follicles

Generally:

  • Hairline adjustment - 1000-1800 follicles
  • Medium area baldness - 1800-2800 follicles
  • Large area baldness - 2800-4000 follicles

The exact number requires a professional examination and assessment by a doctor.

What is the shedding period?

The shedding period is a normal phenomenon after hair transplant surgery:

  • Occurs - Usually 2-6 weeks after surgery
  • Duration - Usually lasts 2-4 weeks
  • Phenomenon - The transplanted hair shafts fall out, but the follicles remain
  • Proportion - About 50-80% of the transplanted hairs may fall out

This is completely normal! The hair shafts fall out because they enter a dormant phase, but the follicles are still alive and will begin to grow new hair after 3-4 months.

Don't worry, this is part of the normal hair growth cycle, and the final result will not be affected.

When will I see the final result?

The hair growth timeline after hair transplant surgery is as follows:

  • 0-3 months - Shedding period, then follicles begin to awaken
  • 3-6 months - New hair begins to grow, you can see initial results
  • 6-9 months - Hair grows thicker and longer, the effect becomes more obvious
  • 9-12 months - Final results are basically visible
  • 12-18 months - Hair continues to thicken and mature

Everyone's hair growth rate is slightly different, but most people can see satisfactory results within 12 months. We will schedule regular follow-ups to monitor your recovery.

Results & Safety

Are hair transplant results permanent?

Yes, hair transplant results are usually permanent!

The key reason is that we extract follicles from the occipital region (the back of the head). The hair follicles in this area are genetically not affected by androgens (DHT), so they do not fall out due to androgenetic alopecia.

After these follicles are transplanted to the balding area, they still retain their original characteristics and will not fall out again. This is the scientific principle behind hair transplant surgery.

However, it should be noted that the original hair (non-transplanted hair) may still fall out due to the progression of androgenetic alopecia. Therefore, we usually recommend combining medication to maintain the original hair.

Is hair transplant surgery safe?

Hair Loss Types & Causes

What are the different types of hair loss?

There are several common types of hair loss:

  • Androgenetic Alopecia - The most common type, affecting both men and women. It's genetic and causes pattern baldness.
  • Telogen Effluvium - Temporary hair loss caused by stress, illness, medication, or hormonal changes.
  • Alopecia Areata - An autoimmune condition that causes patchy hair loss.
  • Traction Alopecia - Hair loss from constant pulling or tension on the hair.
  • Scarring Alopecia - Permanent hair loss due to scarring from inflammation, trauma, or other conditions.

A proper diagnosis is essential to determine the right treatment approach.

What causes hair loss in men and women?

Hair loss can be caused by multiple factors:

  • Genetics - Family history is the most significant factor in pattern baldness.
  • Hormonal changes - DHT (dihydrotestosterone) sensitivity in hair follicles.
  • Stress - Physical or emotional stress can trigger telogen effluvium.
  • Nutritional deficiencies - Iron, zinc, biotin, or protein deficiencies.
  • Medical conditions - Thyroid disorders, scalp infections, etc.
  • Medications - Some drugs can cause hair loss as a side effect.
  • Aging - Hair naturally thins as we age.

Understanding the cause helps determine the most appropriate treatment.

How can I prevent hair loss?

While genetic hair loss cannot be completely prevented, you can slow it down:

  • Balanced diet - Eat protein-rich foods, fruits, and vegetables.
  • Proper hair care - Avoid excessive heat, chemical treatments, and tight hairstyles.
  • Scalp massage - Stimulates blood circulation to the follicles.
  • Stress management - Practice meditation, exercise, or yoga.
  • Medical treatments - FDA-approved medications like minoxidil and finasteride.
  • Avoid smoking - Smoking reduces blood flow to the scalp.
  • Protect from sun - UV rays can damage hair and scalp.

Early intervention yields the best results in preserving existing hair.

Does stress really cause hair loss?

Yes, stress can indeed cause hair loss through several mechanisms:

  • Telogen effluvium - Stress pushes hair follicles into the resting phase prematurely.
  • Trichotillomania - Compulsive hair pulling due to anxiety or stress.
  • Alopecia areata - Stress can trigger or worsen autoimmune hair loss.
  • Hormonal changes - Stress affects cortisol levels which impact hair growth.

Stress-related hair loss is usually temporary. Managing stress can help hair regrow normally within 6-9 months.

Hair Transplant Technology Comparison

What's the difference between FUE, FUT, and microneedle?

Here's a comparison of the main hair transplant methods:

  • FUT (Follicular Unit Transplantation) - Removes a strip of skin from the donor area, leaves a linear scar. Largely outdated now.
  • FUE (Follicular Unit Extraction) - Extracts individual follicles using a punch tool, minimal scarring. Implants using forceps.
  • Microneedle (Implant Pen) - Similar extraction to FUE, but implants using a specialized pen for better precision, smaller incisions, and more natural direction.

Microneedle is generally considered the most advanced technique currently available, offering better results and faster recovery.

What is no-shave hair transplant?

No-shave hair transplant allows you to keep your existing hair:

  • Advantages - No visible signs of surgery, can return to work immediately, more privacy.
  • How it works - Hair is trimmed very short in small sections rather than the entire head.
  • Suitable candidates - Those who want to keep their hair longer, professionals needing privacy, or upcoming events.
  • Considerations - May be more time-consuming, requires more skill from the surgeon.

Barley offers advanced no-shave hair transplant techniques for eligible patients.

What is PRP therapy and how does it help?

PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma) therapy uses your own blood to stimulate hair growth:

  • Process - Blood is drawn, processed to concentrate platelets, then injected into the scalp.
  • How it helps - Growth factors in platelets stimulate follicles, improve blood flow, and strengthen existing hair.
  • Benefits - Natural, minimal downtime, can enhance transplant results.
  • Treatment course - Typically 3-6 sessions spaced 4-6 weeks apart.
  • Best for - Early-stage hair loss, improving transplant survival, or as maintenance.

PRP can be used alone or in combination with hair transplant surgery for enhanced results.

Post-Operative Maintenance

Do I need to take medications after hair transplant?

Medications may be recommended after transplant:

  • Pain relievers - Short-term for post-operative discomfort.
  • Antibiotics - Sometimes prescribed to prevent infection.
  • Anti-inflammatory - To reduce swelling and discomfort.
  • Minoxidil - Can help maintain existing hair and enhance growth.
  • Finasteride - For men, helps prevent further hair loss in native hair.

Always follow your doctor's specific recommendations. Some medications may be optional depending on your individual situation.

Can I color or perm my hair after transplant?

Yes, but you need to wait for proper healing:

  • Hair coloring - Wait at least 4-6 weeks after surgery.
  • Perms or chemical straightening - Wait 8-12 weeks.
  • Why wait - The scalp needs time to heal, and chemicals can irritate or damage grafts.
  • Recommendation - Do a patch test first, and start with gentler products.
  • Maintenance - Continue with gentle, sulfate-free shampoos.

Always consult with your doctor before using any chemical treatments on your transplanted hair.

How do I take care of my scalp long-term?

Long-term scalp care is important for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Gentle cleansing - Use mild, sulfate-free shampoos.
  • Regular washing - Keep scalp clean, don't let oil and dirt accumulate.
  • Scalp massage - Stimulates blood circulation 2-3 times per week.
  • UV protection - Wear hats or use scalp sunscreen in strong sun.
  • Healthy diet - Protein, iron, zinc, biotin, and vitamins A, C, D, E.
  • Stay hydrated - Water is essential for healthy hair.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals - Minimize use of strong styling products.

Regular check-ups with your hair transplant clinic can also help monitor your progress.

Will I need touch-up procedures in the future?

Some patients may benefit from touch-up procedures:

  • Possible reasons - Further thinning of native hair, wanting higher density, or refining the hairline.
  • Typical timeline - If needed, usually 12-18 months after the initial procedure.
  • Donor area - The same donor area can usually be reused if sufficient follicles remain.
  • Considerations - Touch-ups are optional and based on individual goals and progression of hair loss.
  • Prevention - Proper post-op care and maintenance can minimize the need.

Many patients are satisfied with a single procedure, but touch-ups are always an option if desired.

Myths & Misconceptions

Absolutely not! Women can and do get hair transplants:

  • Common female concerns - Widening part, thinning crown, or high hairline.
  • Different approaches - Women often need different techniques than men.
  • Aesthetic goals - Hairline design considerations are different for women.
  • Donor considerations - Women may have different donor area characteristics.

Does hair transplant look artificial?

Modern hair transplants look very natural when done correctly:

  • Natural hairline design - Irregular, age-appropriate patterns.
  • Directional planting - Following natural growth angles and swirls.
  • Density variation - Gradual transitions from dense to sparse.
  • Modern techniques - Microneedle technology allows precise placement.
  • Artistic skill - Experienced surgeons understand aesthetic principles.

When done by skilled surgeons, hair transplants are so natural that even hairdressers can't tell!

Can I use someone else's hair for transplant?

No, hair transplant requires using your own hair for several important reasons:

  • Rejection risk - Your immune system would reject someone else's follicles.
  • Genetic matching - Hair characteristics must match for natural results.
  • Long-term viability - Only your own follicles will survive permanently.
  • Medical safety - Risk of disease transmission from donor hair.

This is why having sufficient donor hair is an important requirement for hair transplant eligibility.

Will transplanted hair fall out over time?

Transplanted hair should be permanent, but there's an important distinction:

  • Transplanted follicles - From the back/sides, genetically resistant to DHT, should be permanent.
  • Original hair - May still thin due to ongoing pattern baldness.
  • Shedding phase - Normal temporary shedding 2-6 weeks post-op.
  • Regrowth - New growth begins at 3-4 months.

This is why combining transplant with medication is often recommended to protect your original, non-transplanted hair.

Is hair transplant painful and dangerous?

Modern hair transplant is very safe and minimally uncomfortable:

  • Local anesthesia - You're awake but the area is numb.
  • Minimal discomfort - Most patients report mild pressure at most.
  • Short recovery - Back to normal activities quickly.
  • Safety record - Millions of successful procedures worldwide.
  • Rare complications - Any issues are usually minor and manageable.

When performed by qualified professionals in a properly equipped facility, hair transplant is considered very safe with minimal risks.

What is Norwood scale and how does it apply to me?

The Norwood scale classifies male pattern baldness:

  • Norwood 3 - Significant recession, early intervention ideal
  • Norwood 4-5 - Moderate to extensive baldness
  • Norwood 6-7 - Extensive baldness, more challenging
  • Individual assessment - Your doctor will assess your specific pattern

The scale helps determine the best treatment approach and expected outcomes for your specific situation.

Women's hair loss can have multiple triggers:

  • Genetics - Family history still plays a role
  • Hormonal changes - Pregnancy, menopause, thyroid issues
  • Nutritional deficiencies - Iron, zinc, biotin, protein deficiencies
  • Stress - Telogen effluvium from physical or emotional stress
  • Traction - Tight hairstyles causing traction alopecia
  • Medical conditions - Various health conditions can affect hair

Proper diagnosis is important to determine the right treatment approach for women's hair loss.

Medical Treatments & Medications

Should I use minoxidil before or after transplant?

Minoxidil (Rogaine) can be beneficial:

  • Before transplant - Can help strengthen existing hair
  • After healing - Can start 2-4 weeks post-op
  • Benefits - Helps maintain existing hair, may enhance growth
  • Continuous use - Needs ongoing use for sustained benefit
  • Doctor guidance - Follow your surgeon's specific recommendations

Minoxidil is often recommended as part of a comprehensive hair maintenance program.

What about finasteride (Propecia) for men?

Finasteride can be effective for preventing further loss:

  • DHT blocker - Reduces DHT, the main hormone causing loss
  • Preservation - Helps maintain existing, non-transplanted hair
  • Before transplant - Often recommended to stabilize loss first
  • After transplant - Continues to protect native hair
  • Considerations - Discuss potential side effects with doctor

Finasteride is prescription-only and should be discussed with a qualified physician.

Are there natural alternatives for hair loss?

Some natural options may help, though evidence varies:

  • Supplements - Biotin, saw palmetto, pumpkin seed oil
  • Scalp massage - Improves blood circulation
  • Essential oils - Rosemary, peppermint, lavender (patch test first)
  • Nutrition - Protein, iron, zinc, vitamins A, C, D, E
  • Stress reduction - Meditation, exercise, adequate sleep
  • Evidence limitation - Most lack strong clinical evidence

Natural approaches may complement medical treatments but generally aren't substitutes for proven therapies.

Can vitamins and supplements help my results?

Proper nutrition supports healthy hair growth:

  • Protein - Hair is primarily protein, ensure adequate intake
  • Iron - Deficiency can cause shedding and poor growth
  • Zinc - Important for follicle health and repair
  • Biotin - Supports keratin production and hair strength
  • Vitamins - A, C, D, E all play roles in hair health
  • Balanced diet - Whole foods better than isolated supplements

While good nutrition supports health, supplements won't compensate for poor surgical technique or planning.
