Finasteride: How It Works

Understanding the science behind the most effective oral treatment for male pattern baldness. Discover how finasteride blocks DHT, the primary hormone responsible for hair loss, and stops balding at its source.

What is Finasteride?

The FDA-approved oral medication for male pattern baldness

Finasteride is an oral medication used to treat male pattern baldness (androgenetic alopecia). Sold under brand names like Propecia® (1mg) for hair loss and Proscar® (5mg) for enlarged prostate, finasteride works by targeting the root cause of genetic hair loss: DHT.

Originally developed to treat benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH), researchers discovered that men taking finasteride experienced significant hair regrowth. This led to FDA approval for hair loss treatment in 1997. Today, it remains one of the most effective and well-researched treatments for male pattern baldness.

Key Facts About Finasteride

  • FDA-approved: First oral treatment for male pattern baldness
  • Prescription-only: Requires doctor's prescription
  • For men only: Not approved for women or children
  • Dosage: 1mg daily for hair loss treatment
  • DHT blocker: Reduces scalp DHT by ~60-70%
  • Highly effective: 80-90% stop hair loss, 60-70% see regrowth

The Science: DHT & Hair Loss

Understanding the hormone that causes genetic balding

What is DHT?

Dihydrotestosterone - the hormone behind male pattern baldness.

  • Potent androgen (male hormone)
  • Formed from testosterone by 5-alpha-reductase
  • Causes hair follicles to miniaturize
  • Shorter growth cycles, thinner hairs
  • Genetically susceptible follicles affected

5-Alpha-Reductase

The enzyme that converts testosterone to DHT.

  • Type I & Type II isoenzymes
  • Type II primarily in hair follicles
  • Finasteride targets Type II (and Type I weakly)
  • Blocks conversion process
  • Reduces DHT levels systemically

Follicle Miniaturization

How DHT causes hair follicles to shrink.

  • DHT binds to androgen receptors in follicles
  • Shortens anagen (growth) phase
  • Extends telogen (resting) phase
  • Follicles produce thinner hair shafts
  • Eventually follicles stop producing visible hair

Genetic Susceptibility

Why some men lose hair and others don't.

  • Inherited androgen receptor sensitivity
  • Follicles programmed to respond to DHT
  • Can inherit from mother or father
  • Usually develops in 20s-30s
  • Progressive without treatment

How Finasteride Blocks DHT

The mechanism of action explained

The Mechanism of Action

  • Competitive inhibition: Finasteride binds to 5-alpha-reductase enzyme
  • Blocks conversion: Prevents testosterone from being converted to DHT
  • Type II specific: Primarily targets Type II isoenzyme in hair follicles
  • Reduces scalp DHT: Lowers DHT levels in the scalp by ~60-70%
  • Systemic reduction: Also lowers serum DHT by ~70%
  • Maintains testosterone: Testosterone levels remain in normal range

What This Means for Hair

  • Stops miniaturization: Follicles no longer shrink from DHT exposure
  • Normalizes growth cycle: Anagen phase returns to normal length
  • Prevents further loss: Existing hairs are protected from DHT damage
  • Allows recovery: Miniaturized follicles can recover and produce thicker hairs
  • Gradual improvement: Results appear over 6-12 months of consistent use
  • Requires maintenance: Must continue to maintain results

Clinical Evidence & Effectiveness

What research shows about finasteride's results

Study Results

Extensive research confirms finasteride's effectiveness.

  • 80-90% of men stop hair loss
  • 60-70% experience visible regrowth
  • Best results in vertex/crown region
  • Also effective for frontal recession
  • Results statistically significant vs placebo

Results Timeline

Patience and consistency are essential.

  • 1-3 months: Slowing or stopping of hair loss
  • 3-6 months: Noticeable stabilization
  • 6-9 months: Visible regrowth begins
  • 9-12 months: 60-80% of final results
  • 12-24 months: Peak results achieved

Ideal Candidates

Who benefits most from finasteride.

  • Men aged 18-41 with male pattern baldness
  • Early to moderate hair loss (Norwood 2-5)
  • Vertex/crown thinning (best response)
  • Recent onset (under 5 years)
  • Good overall health
  • Understanding of side effect profile

Dosage & Administration

How to take finasteride correctly.

  • Dosage: 1mg tablet once daily
  • Timing: Same time each day (consistency matters)
  • With or without food: Either is fine
  • Missed dose: Take when remembered, skip if close to next dose
  • Don't double dose: Never take two tablets at once

Safety & Side Effects

What to know about finasteride's safety profile

Most Common & Mild

Side effects are rare and usually mild.

  • Decreased libido (1-2%)
  • Erectile dysfunction (1-2%)
  • Decreased ejaculate volume (1-2%)
  • Usually resolve with continued use
  • Go away completely if stopped

Less Common Effects

Rare but possible side effects.

  • Breast tenderness or enlargement
  • Skin rash or allergic reaction
  • Testicular pain
  • Mood changes (rare)
  • Headache (rare)

Important Considerations

Critical safety information.

  • NOT for women: Especially dangerous during pregnancy
  • NOT for children: Never use in anyone under 18
  • Pregnancy risk: Can cause birth defects in male fetuses
  • Don't handle crushed tablets: If pregnant or potentially pregnant
  • PSA levels: Affects prostate cancer screening tests

Monitoring & Follow-up

What to expect during treatment.

  • Initial consultation with doctor
  • Baseline PSA test (if over 40)
  • Follow-up at 6-12 months
  • Report any concerning side effects
  • Regular prostate cancer screening as advised

FAQ About Finasteride

Common questions about finasteride treatment

Can I use finasteride with minoxidil?

Absolutely! In fact, this combination is highly recommended:

  • Complementary mechanisms: Finasteride blocks DHT (the cause), minoxidil stimulates growth
  • Better results: Studies show combination therapy more effective than either alone
  • No interactions: They work through different pathways, no negative interactions
  • Synergistic: Attack hair loss from two different angles

This is considered the gold-standard medical treatment for male pattern baldness by most hair restoration specialists. They work beautifully together!

What happens if I stop taking finasteride?

Unfortunately, the benefits are not permanent - you'll gradually lose what you gained:

  • DHT levels return to baseline within 2 weeks
  • Hair loss resumes at its natural rate
  • Any regrown hair will shed within 6-12 months
  • Typically returns to where you would have been without treatment
  • Transplanted hair is NOT affected

Finasteride is a treatment, not a cure. It needs to be used continuously to maintain results. If you're considering stopping, consult with a specialist first!

Can I use finasteride after a hair transplant?

Yes! In fact, it's often strongly recommended:

  • Protects your existing non-transplanted hair from further loss
  • Helps maintain your overall hair density
  • Transplanted hairs are permanent (genetically resistant to DHT)
  • But native surrounding hairs are still vulnerable
  • Can start 1-2 weeks post-op (follow surgeon's advice)

Many surgeons consider finasteride an essential part of protecting your hair transplant investment. It ensures you don't continue losing your native hair after the procedure!

How soon will I see results from finasteride?

Results take time - be patient and consistent:

  • 1-3 months: You may notice less shedding (first positive sign!)
  • 3-6 months: Hair loss stabilizes, no further progression
  • 6-9 months: Fine new hairs may become visible
  • 9-12 months: Noticeable thickening and regrowth
  • 12-24 months: Peak results achieved

Don't judge results before 12 months! Finasteride works slowly but effectively. Take monthly photos to track your progress - the changes will be noticeable over time!

What about the sexual side effects I've heard about?

Let's put this in perspective with actual data:

  • Clinical trials show 1-2% experience sexual side effects (similar to placebo)
  • Most are mild and temporary: Resolve with continued use or upon stopping
  • No permanent effects proven: Despite internet rumors, no conclusive evidence
  • Psychological factor: Anxiety can contribute - don't self-diagnose
  • Discuss with doctor: They can help assess your personal risk

The vast majority of men take finasteride with no issues at all. If you do experience side effects, they almost always go away completely if you stop the medication. Don't let fear prevent you from considering this highly effective treatment!

Can I take the 5mg finasteride (Proscar) and cut it?

Yes, this is common and safe when done correctly:

  • 5mg Proscar tablets are identical to Propecia, just higher dosage
  • Cutting into quarters gives approximately 1.25mg per piece
  • This is very close to the recommended 1mg dosage
  • Can save significant money compared to brand-name Propecia
  • Important: Use a pill splitter for accurate dosing
  • Important: Don't use if tablets are crushed or broken

Many men do this successfully! Just make sure to discuss it with your doctor first, and use a proper pill splitter to get consistent dosages.
