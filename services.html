FAQ About Hair Transplant Procedures

Common questions about our hair restoration services and procedures

What's the difference between hairline transplant and hair thickening?

While both procedures use similar techniques, they serve different purposes:

Hairline Transplant:

  • Focuses on creating or restoring the frontal hairline
  • Requires precise artistic design for natural appearance
  • Typically uses 1000-1800 follicular units
  • Emphasizes natural, irregular patterns and direction

Hair Thickening:

  • Adds density to existing but thinning areas
  • Distributed throughout the sparse regions
  • Requires careful placement between existing hairs
  • Can use more follicles depending on the area size

Many patients combine both procedures for comprehensive restoration.

Is eyebrow transplant different from scalp hair transplant?

Yes, eyebrow transplantation requires special techniques and considerations:

  • Finer Grafts - Requires single hair follicles for natural appearance
  • Precise Angles - Eyebrows grow at specific angles that must be matched
  • Artistic Design - Requires careful shaping to complement facial features
  • Different Growth Pattern - Transplanted hairs grow like scalp hair and need trimming
  • Fewer Grafts Needed - Typically 100-300 grafts per eyebrow
  • More Delicate Procedure - Requires greater precision and surgical skill

Our doctors specialize in facial hair restoration with extensive experience in eyebrow transplants.

How soon can I see results from PRP therapy?

PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma) therapy results appear gradually:

  • First Month - Improved scalp health, reduced shedding may be noticed
  • 2-3 Months - Hair follicles begin strengthening, new growth may appear
  • 3-6 Months - Increased hair density and thickness become visible
  • 6-12 Months - Full results are typically seen

A typical PRP treatment course involves 3-6 sessions spaced 4-6 weeks apart. Results vary by individual, and maintenance treatments may be recommended for sustained benefits.

Who is a good candidate for beard transplantation?

Beard transplantation is suitable for men with:

  • Patchy Beard Growth - Areas with sparse or no hair growth
  • Scarring - Facial scars that prevent hair growth
  • Genetic Sparse Growth - Naturally thin or incomplete beard pattern
  • Transgender Patients - Seeking facial masculinization
  • Enough Donor Hair - Sufficient healthy hair on the scalp
  • Realistic Expectations - Understanding what can be achieved

The procedure can fill in gaps, create full beards, goatees, mustaches, or sideburns depending on your goals and available donor hair.

What's the recovery like for scar hair transplant?

Scar hair transplantation has slightly different recovery considerations:

  • Scar Assessment - The scar must have sufficient blood supply to support transplanted follicles
  • Initial Healing - 7-10 days for the area to heal
  • Gentle Care - The scar area may be more sensitive and require gentler handling
  • Slower Growth - Hair may grow slightly slower through scar tissue
  • Multiple Sessions - Some scars require 2-3 sessions for optimal coverage
  • Success Rate - Generally good when the scar is properly assessed pre-operatively

Our doctors carefully evaluate each scar to determine the best approach and expected outcome before proceeding.

Can I combine multiple procedures in one session?

Yes, many procedures can be combined for comprehensive results:

Common Combinations:

  • Hairline + Crown - Address both frontal recession and crown thinning
  • Transplant + PRP - Surgery with PRP for enhanced healing and growth
  • Hairline + Eyebrows - Complete facial hair restoration
  • Beard + Sideburns - Complete facial hair transformation
  • Transplant + Scalp Micro-pigmentation - For the appearance of additional density

However, the total number of grafts needed and your available donor hair will determine what can be safely combined in a single session. Your doctor will help create the optimal treatment plan for you.

What causes androgenetic alopecia (genetic hair loss)?

Androgenetic alopecia is the most common form of hair loss:

  • Genetic Predisposition - Inherited sensitivity to DHT (dihydrotestosterone)
  • Hormonal Influence - DHT causes hair follicles to miniaturize over time
  • Progressive Condition - Hair loss worsens gradually if untreated
  • Pattern Baldness - Follows distinct patterns (receding hairline, crown thinning)
  • Both Genders Affected - Men and women can experience genetic hair loss

While you can't change your genetics, hair transplantation can permanently restore hair in affected areas using DHT-resistant follicles from the donor region.

What is seborrheic dermatitis and can it cause hair loss?

Seborrheic dermatitis is a common scalp condition:

  • Symptoms - Redness, itching, flaking, and scaling of the scalp
  • Causes - Yeast overgrowth, genetics, and environmental factors
  • Temporary Shedding - Can cause increased hair shedding, not permanent loss
  • Scalp Health - Important to manage before hair transplantation
  • Treatments Available - Medicated shampoos, topical treatments, and lifestyle adjustments

We can help you manage seborrheic dermatitis and improve scalp health before any hair restoration procedure.

What are the benefits of body hair transplantation?

Body hair transplantation expands your restoration options:

  • Additional Donor Supply - Uses chest, back, or leg hair when scalp donor is limited
  • More Coverage - Allows for more extensive restoration than scalp-only donation
  • Natural Results - Can create excellent density when properly matched
  • Alternative for Severe Loss - Great option for patients with advanced baldness
  • Combined Approach - Often used with scalp hair for optimal results

Our doctors are experienced in body hair transplantation and can assess if this is a good option for your specific situation.

  • Age-Appropriate - Considers your age and style preferences

Sideburns frame the face—our doctors take special care to ensure they look completely natural and enhance your appearance.

What is the difference between FUE and microneedle hair transplant?

While both are excellent, microneedle offers significant advantages:

  • Implantation Method - Microneedle uses implant pen vs forceps placement
  • Incision Size - Microneedle: 0.6-1.0mm vs traditional FUE: 1.0-1.5mm
  • Recovery Time - Microneedle: 2-3 days vs traditional: 5-7 days
  • Density Potential - Microneedle can achieve 20-30% higher density
  • Follicle Survival - Less trauma = higher survival rate with microneedle
  • Natural Direction - 360° rotation capability for perfect angle matching

This is why Barley pioneered and exclusively uses microneedle technology for superior patient outcomes.

Can I get a hair transplant if I'm still losing hair?

Yes, but with important considerations:

  • Stability Assessment - We evaluate if your hair loss has stabilized
  • Age Factor - Younger patients may need to wait for loss patterns to clarify
  • Medical Therapy - We often recommend medications to stabilize loss
  • Future Planning - Design conservatively to accommodate future loss
  • Staged Procedures - Multiple sessions may be recommended over time

Our doctors will assess your individual situation and create a treatment plan that addresses both your current needs and future considerations.

What are realistic expectations for crown restoration?

Crown restoration has unique considerations:

  • Whirl Pattern - Must recreate the natural crown swirl pattern
  • Density Requirements - Crowns often need more grafts for full coverage
  • Light Reflection - Scalp shows through more easily in crown area
  • Multiple Sessions - May require 2+ procedures for optimal density
  • Donor Planning - Needs careful donor hair management
  • Gradual Improvement - Results build over 12-18 months

Our doctors are experts in crown restoration and will create a realistic plan based on your specific donor hair availability and goals.
