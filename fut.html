Why Barley FUT

Advanced FUT technology combined with our microneedle expertise

In recent decades, hair transplant technology and surgical methods have advanced at an exponential rate. FUT, which was a major breakthrough in the hair restoration industry, is one of the most commonly used surgical hair transplant methods for extracting grafts for hair restoration.

Follicular Unit Transplant (FUT) involves surgically removing a thin strip of hair from the donor area of the back of the head, where the hair is healthy and strong. Grafts are then extracted from this hair strip, i.e., hair follicles are separated and implanted in the recipient area where hair is needed.

Although the procedure leaves a linear scar in the incision area, it is barely visible to the naked eye.

Our FUT differs from traditional FUT in that we combine it with our microneedle technology to maximize the number of grafts. This is beneficial when a client has thin or sparse hair in the donor area.

As a result, we only use common FUT on rare occasions, and only when our clients have thick and wavy hair that is suitable for FUT. The combination of FUT and our microneedle methods has produced excellent results for our clients.

More About FUT Hair Transplant

Understanding the strip harvesting method and its benefits

FUT is also known as the strip harvesting method because a thin strip of hair is used to harvest healthy and strong grafts, and it is a reliable method because it ensures natural and dense hair regeneration in the recipient area.

FUT is typically recommended for people with dense, thick, and wavy hair because it provides enough hair to extract a strip of hair for transplant. In our practice at Barley, all procedures are planned and carried out under the supervision of an experienced Barley doctor, who first consults with the clients to determine the best course of action for the FUT follicle extraction surgery.

The FUT Procedure

Three key stages of the Follicular Unit Transplant process

Donor Area Preparation And Harvesting

At this stage, the donor area is prepared for graft harvesting, and our hair experts remove a thin strip of tissue from the donor area. The strip is removed through a surgical incision. First, the hair is trimmed, and then a small strip is removed with expert precision. This entire procedure is delicate and necessitates expertise to ensure that there is minimal scarring and damage to the donor area.

Graft Preparation And Follicle Extraction

The strip is trimmed after extraction to obtain and separate graft units. A graft unit is made up of one to four hair follicles that are separated and prepared for hair transplant. Technical expertise is required to ensure that the harvested grafts remain healthy throughout the procedure so that these follicles can be placed in the hairline created during the first stages of the FUT procedure.

Recovery & Results

Any post-FUT discomfort and incision marks will fade within a week, and no special care is required. Our hair experts at Barley make certain that our clients receive proper aftercare advice and will assist you in this regard. It usually takes 3 to 6 months to see a visible difference and for the transplanted hair to begin regenerating. A hair transplant is a permanent solution to any hair loss problem you may be experiencing.

FAQ for International Patients

Common questions about FUT hair transplant for our international patients

What is the difference between FUT and FUE?

The main difference is in how grafts are harvested:

  • FUT (Strip Method) - Removes a thin strip of tissue from the donor area, then extracts grafts from the strip. Leaves a thin linear scar.
  • FUE - Extracts individual follicular units one by one using a punch device. Leaves tiny, dot-like scars.

At Barley, we often combine FUT harvesting with our microneedle implantation technology for the best of both approaches when appropriate.

Will the FUT scar be visible?

Modern FUT techniques result in very fine scars that are typically barely noticeable:

  • Thin scar - Our skilled surgeons create extremely fine incisions
  • Hair coverage - The scar is hidden by surrounding hair growth
  • Healing time - The scar fades significantly over weeks and months
  • Position - Located in an area easily covered by hairstyles

Most patients find that the scar is not visible to others when their hair is grown to normal length, even with short hairstyles.

How many grafts can be harvested with FUT?

FUT can typically harvest more grafts in a single session:

  • Standard session - 1,500-3,000 grafts commonly harvested
  • Large sessions - Up to 4,000+ grafts possible with good donor hair
  • Density advantage - Often provides higher overall density than multiple FUE sessions
  • Barley advantage - Our microneedle technology maximizes graft utilization

The exact number depends on your individual donor hair characteristics and goals. Your doctor will assess this during consultation.

What is the recovery time for FUT?

FUT recovery timeline:

  • First week - Initial healing, sutures or staples removed at 7-10 days
  • 1-2 weeks - Most discomfort subsides, can return to light work
  • 2-4 weeks - Resume normal activities, including exercise
  • 3-6 months - Transplanted hair begins growing visibly
  • 9-12 months - Full results visible

Most international patients plan for 10-14 days in China for the procedure and initial recovery.

Is FUT suitable for everyone?

FUT is particularly suitable for:

  • Thick, wavy hair - Ideal candidates have dense donor hair
  • Large restoration needs - Excellent for extensive baldness
  • Maximizing grafts - When you want the most grafts possible in one session
  • Cost considerations - Often more cost-effective per graft

At Barley, we evaluate each patient individually and recommend the best approach (FUT, FUE, or combination) based on your specific situation and goals.
